Sprulina <\/strong><\/p>\n\n\n\n

It\u2019s an extremely healthy nutritional supplement with not one single man-made chemical in it. It\u2019s also very easy to digest, and gives you a feeling of fullness<\/strong> for a rather long time.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Millions of people all over the world use spirulina as a dietary supplement, following the recommendations of the United Nations<\/strong> (UN) and the World Health Organization<\/strong><\/p>\n\n\n\n

it improves your memory <\/strong><\/p>\n\n\n\n

Eating spirulina can help improve your memory capacity. This is because it protects your cognitive systems and stimulates your brain and nervous system<\/strong>. It\u2019s also great for your general well-being. Both of those things can help keep your mood stable and lower your stress.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

It gives you energy<\/strong> & relaxes your brain<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Eating some every day can make you feel less tired<\/strong>. That\u2019s why it\u2019s good for both athletic people and people who don\u2019t exercise much but are trying to start. It can help everyone push themselves and make their training much more efficient and productive.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

One of the main ingredients in spirulina is tryptophan, which synthesizes other proteins and balances out your nervous system. Tryptophan is an amino acid that\u2019s also the key to your body\u2019s production of serotonin<\/a>, the happiness hormone. Because it\u2019s necessary for serotonin, it helps regulate mood, stress, and appetite.<\/strong><\/p>\n\n\n\n

You also need tryptophan to produce melatonin<\/a> in your body. Melatonin is the main hormone for regulating your sleep cycles, so it\u2019s an important part of getting good sleep.<\/strong><\/p>\n\n\n\n

Dark chocolate<\/strong><\/p>\n\n\n\n

Are you craving a luscious way to keep your brain at its best? Reach for some dark chocolate. A study published in the Neuroscience & Biobehavioral Reviews <\/em>journal found that dark chocolate is a superfood due to its high levels of flavanols. These are special compounds that have been shown to help improve blood flow to the brain, which can increase cognitive function and memory. Another reason to feel good about enjoying dark chocolate is because it has an abundance of polyphenols. These are unique phytochemicals that have been shown to help reduce depression and anxiety. Just remember to choose dark chocolate with at least 70% cacao and try to indulge in no more than two squares a day. So now when you have some dark chocolate, remember that you\u2019re also giving your brain a sweet treat.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Beets make you smart & young<\/p>\n\n\n\n

The humble beet has been found to boost your gray matter during exercise, thanks to its high levels of oxygen-enhancing nitrates, researchers at Wake Forest University found. In a study, 26 middle-aged men and women with high blood pressure drank either beet juice or a placebo before exercising \u2014 and the ones who got the beets, had much higher levels of nitric oxide, which increases blood and oxygen flow in the body.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

“Nitric oxide is a really powerful molecule,” said study coauthor W. Jack Rejeski.<\/a> “It goes to the areas of the body which are hypoxic, or needing oxygen, and the brain is a heavy feeder of oxygen in your body.”<\/p>\n\n\n\n
