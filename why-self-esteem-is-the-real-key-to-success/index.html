Why Self Esteem Is The REAL Key To Success

What is self-admiration? Is it the hidden key to success? And what does admiring your own self mean anyway? Find out the answers in this detailed guide discussing the ultimate secret to achieving more in life.

If you ask people what the secret is for living a successful life, you will come across a variety of answers such as identifying your goals, pursuing your passion, developing a plan of action, being consistent in your efforts, perseverance, and so on.

But if you asksuccessfulpeople what enabled them to reach where they are and live the life they had always envisioned, you are likely to find most of them answering something along the lines of believing in yourself and being confident in your abilities.

Finding the Hidden Key to Success

Take a hint from the success stories of famous personalities like Oprah Winfrey and J.K. Rowling. Despite being born into a low-income family, Winfrey is known by millions of people all over the world today as a billionaire philanthropist. Rowling, at one point in her life, was living penny-to-penny, and now, she is one of the wealthiest people in the world.

What did they have in common that made them so wildly successful?

They didnt give up on their dreams!

They believed that they could turn their lives around and have whatever their hearts desired. And thats precisely what they did!

Self-confidence. Thats where they started.

And it all begins with developing a sense of self-admiration i.e., believing that you have the power to change your life regardless of what your current circumstances might be forcing you to think otherwise.

Whether you are on the road to self-improvement, trying to find the hidden key to success, or wondering if a lack of confidence affects your quality of life, this post is for you.

In this guide to life freedom, we discuss the following aspects of self-admiration:

  • What is self-admiration?
  • What are the benefits of self-admiration?
  • How to gain freedom in life through self-admiration?

Ready to turn your dreams into a reality and live the life you have always wanted?

Lets begin by taking a more in-depth look at what self-admiration is and shattering some commonly held but false beliefs about this feeling of self-love.

What Is Self Admiration?

Well, in the simplest terms, self-admiration is just what you would generally understand by the individual words that it is comprised of admiring yourself.

Self-admiration is the feeling of being pleased with yourself. It is thinking well of yourself and taking pride in who you are.

However, the concept of self-admiration can be quite confusing at times. This is because the term self-admiration tends to mislead people into viewing the idea under a negative light.

Self Admiration What It Is NOT

Being fond of oneself is usually notorious for leading to disastrous results. This is because self-admiration is generally considered to be synonymous with conceitedness, self-centeredness, egocentrism, and arrogance.

While a thesaurus might approve of this notion, keep in mind that, in reality, self-admiration is NOT the same as any of these ideas.

Some scholars have written books about how self-admiration is a disease of the soul as it is responsible for leading people into sin. They believe that self-admiration tricks you into a false sense of self-righteousness. It deludes you into thinking that you are perfect. So, you stop striving towards betterment and ultimately fall into a stagnant lifestyle.

It would be incorrect to say that they are entirely wrong in their stance because if practiced at extremely high levels, self-admiration can indeed lead to such results.

However, self-admiration, in essence, is not a bad thing. Basically, there is a very thin line that distinguishes self-admiration from other harmful behaviors like self-obsession, self-absorption, self-centeredness, and selfishness itself.

It is essential to understand this if you want to be more successful in life.

So, What Does Admiring Yourself Really Mean?

Self-admiration is linked with building self-esteem. It is about celebrating your individuality, or in simple words, feelingconfidentin your own skin.

It is the belief that you are not just worthy of receiving good things in life, but more so, that you are perfectly capable of achieving them on your own if you want.

Self-admiration is what empowers you to attain excellence. It motivates you to strive towards your goals in a way that you are bound to be successful in your endeavors.

Admiring yourself has nothing to do with good looks. Rather, it entails embracing yourself wholeheartedly imperfections and all.

Self-admiration means that you appreciate your uniqueness and what makes you, you.

Why Self-Admiration Is the Hidden Key to Success The Benefits of Being Your Own Admirer

Everyone feels proud of themselves whenever they are successful in attaining what they had set out to achieve. Self-admiration means that you take pride in the so-called failures as well.

When you stop being too hard on yourself and realize that mistakes ultimately help you improve, you are able to push past your fear of failure and work towards your goals, feeling more confident in your skills. This further increases your chances of success.

There are many reasons why self-admiration is the hidden key to success:

  • It makes you optimistic about the future
  • It helps you put in your best efforts as you realize that you have the ability to accomplish your goals
  • Self-admiration encourages you to treat yourself kindly
  • It motivates you to take better care of yourself. Nurturing self-love is important as it helps you eat healthier and stay in good shape by maintaining an active lifestyle
  • Admiring yourself and your achievements in life so far automatically uplift your mood and make you feel energized. This, in turn, increases your overall satisfaction in life
  • Self-admiration helps you break free from unproductive habits such as procrastinating by motivating you to get things done on time
  • It enables you to maintain a positive outlook on life even if things seem pretty bleak. This is crucial for living thats full of joy always and not dictated by factors that were out of your control anyway
  • It makes you more grateful by instilling a sense of appreciation for everything in life even the apparently bad ones
  • Self-admiration strengthens your relationships with others
  • It builds your interpersonal skills as others feel attracted to your amiable personality

How to Gain Freedom in Life Self-Admiration Made Easy

Still not sure how self-admiration can help you navigate your way on the road to success? Or did you get the idea but are confused about implementing it in the right manner to get the desired results?

Whatever the case might be, carefully read through the following steps to develop a deeper insight into how appreciating and believing in yourself can take you to greater heights.

Be True to Yourself

An important step in learning to be comfortable in your own skin is to stop pretending and doing things for the sake of making others happy.

More often than not, living a happier, more purposeful life is not that difficult, but we tend to complicate things for ourselves. Trying to please others might help you win the love of your life or appear good in the eyes of others temporarily. However, the relationships you form on that basis are bound to be hollow, and so, will fail to withstand the test of time.

Being more successful is all about growing and evolving into the person you are capable of being. And you cannot reach there by trying to be like someone else.

As Oscar Wilde famously said, Be yourself, because everyone else is already taken.

Plus, an original is always worth more than a copy.

When you start appreciating your own worth, the world naturally follows suit.

Let It Go

One of the best ways to leverage the power of self-admiration for being more successful in life is to identify and deal with the root causes of low self-esteem.

Perhaps you have always been the target of criticism from your family members. Maybe your parents style of upbringing led you into believing that you arent good enough. Or perhaps you used to be a confident person until you stepped into the corporate world and had to deal with a lot of complaints from your manager or your clients.

No matter what the main reason might be, you need to raise your self-esteem back up again.

Remind yourself that what others think of you is none of your business. No one can make you feel inferior without your consent. Harsh remarks and rude comments cannot cause you any harm until you give them the power to.

And if its about your job performance, or if its truly constructive criticism, take the feedback with an open mind. Try to see things from the other persons perspective. This helps make critical work for you.

Get Things Done

Maybe you want to start your own business, buy a big house, or establish a school for the underprivileged children, and so on. If your own goal seems to be too far from reality, theres no need to be disheartened. Rather than feeling overwhelmed by the big picture, focus on taking small steps instead.

While others can surely support you in your mission, it is important to realize that the power to accomplish that goal lies with you only.

Break down bigger tasks into smaller, actionable steps. When you are able to complete the initial steps successfully, you will feel more confident in your abilities. It then provides a strong point for growing your self-belief even further.

Disclaimer: Self Admiration is Healthy, Narcissism Is Not

When you hear the word self-admiration, do you picture a person looking at themselves in a mirror admiringly?

If you answered yes, you are not alone. And you are also not right.

This type of self-love is tilted more towards what is commonly known as narcissism.

Becoming your own admirer doesnt mean that you spend hours gazing at your reflection each day. Nor does it mean that looking at your own image should be a source of utmost delight.

Of course, acknowledging that you are no less than an awesome person is part of the idea, but that surely isnt what self-admiration is all about.

Self-admiration focuses on utilizing your inner strengths rather than your physical goodness to unlock the door to new opportunities. Narcissism, on the other hand, means that you are more concerned with the way you look rather than how well you perform. In a way, it makes you more dependent on others because you become reliant on external validation to work harder.

However, self-admiration lets you internalize a sense of worthiness. So, whether or not you get the praise you deserve, you always feel good about yourself anyway.

Put simply, self-admiration is healthy; narcissism is not. Period!

End Note

In the words of Prem Pal Rawat, When there can be that admiration for the self, the rest is very, very simple.

Rawat is an Indian American who earned the Global Peace Ambassador award for his works promoting human welfare through self-knowledge and self-improvement.

This alone is a solid proof that self-admiration is indeed the hidden key to success.

Self-admiration helps you realize your inner talents, making you feel more confident in pursuing your goals in a way that yields the desired results.

Just keep in mind that self-obsession and self-admiration are not the different sides of the same coin the two are completely poles apart. The former will deter you from the road to success by making you arrogant and conceited, whereas the latter will allow you to effectively work towards bringing a change for good. So, start being your own cheerleader and witness how it empowers you to bring your A-game!
